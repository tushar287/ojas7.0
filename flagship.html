Finding Neo is the Flagship event of SPJIMR’s cultural and management fest, OJAS, currently in its 7th version. The event is aimed at finding a multi-faceted manager who can leverage on the pressures of the business world and emerge as a winner.

Ojas 7.0 and Biziga Simulation present before you “Kurukshetra”-Industry Inc., a unique game designed to test the ability of the participants to come up with a strategy that will help their company successfully compete in an ever-changing business environment. Participants should possess the skills of both Krishna and Shakuni to predict the outcome of their chosen strategy based on the actions of their fellow “Pratiyōgiyōṁ”(competitors) and “Sahayōgī” (collaborators) as well as the market forces.

After all, success in today’s business environment does not depend solely on the company’s own performance!

The event comprises of three rounds:
  • Round 1
    • Teams have to register for the event via the Ojas website as well as Dare2Compete.com
    • There will be an online quiz via dare2compete.com comprising contemporary business and finance topics.
    • There will be 20 Questions to be solved in 10 mins
    • Top 6 Maharathis with the maximum points will qualify (In case of a tie the teams with the quickest answer will qualify)
    • The 6 Maharathis will be invited over to SPJIMR “Ranbhoomi” for the next 2 rounds
  • Round 2
    • It will not be battle of Good Vs Evil, instead it will be battle between the Good, The Better and The Best. 6 Teams – 6 grueling rounds of simulation- A battle like no other!!!
  • Round 3
    • If you have not been able to conquer your enemies in the 2nd round, you can still triumph over them by solving a case study and making a presentation thereby winning the “Mahayudh”. Case study ppt of maximum 5 slides and 10 minutes per team for presentation. Cumulative scores at the end of round 3 wins.

  • Any participating team must comprise of 2-3 members.

  • All the members must be from the same institute.
  • There is no restriction on the number of teams that can participate from any institute.
  • The decision of the organizers will be final and binding.
  • The Terms and Conditions of participation are subject to change at any time without specific notice.
  • The amended Terms and Conditions of entry will be effective immediately upon being posted on the website.
  • It is the sole responsibility of the teams to regularly visit the Website to keep themselves informed about changes if any.

Theme for this Year: The theme for this year is "Inclusion". As time passes by, we see there are two India’s emerging, urban and rural, with disparity between them increasing each passing second. Rural India while home to majority of the population, contributes very little to the GDP. It lags behind its Urban brother be it in Banking, Mobile Penetration or Internet Penetration. All this while Tier-I cities come under strain and are beginning to saturate. As India wakes up to the importance of Inclusive growth and its impact on the social fabric, This OJAS... Let’s find out ways to grow together.

Challenge: We would like participants to give us innovative ideas which corporates can undertake to improve the quality of rural facilities and infrastructure for the benefit of rural India. What must you do?

  1. Identify the major problems which are causing the rural-urban divide. These problems can be spread across the areas which you think are important. You are expected to choose broad areas like Telecom (TMT), Consumer Goods, Financial Services, IT, Infrastructure etc
  2. Suggest solutions for rural inclusiveness in the area that you have selected.
  3. The submission must include the problems faced by rural areas in this sector and feasible solutions. Quality and depth of solutions will form the primary basis for evaluation, along with the issues identified and the overall approach taken.
  4. You may choose to explore multiple problems across multiple sectors or deep dive into one sector and provide solutions for that sector alone. This is left to you!
  5. This time, MPower is an open-ended challenge and we would like students to think of creative means to solve this problem.

Concept: The purpose of conducting MPower is to utilize the creativity and business acumen of Indian students to come up with sustainable and tangible solutions to the social issues of India.

The event comprises of two rounds:
  • Round 1
    • Participating teams would need to submit a Power Point presentation of 8 slides (including an executive summary and excluding the cover page). The presentation should cover an overview of the suggested ideas and solution. The presentations need to be mail to ojas.mpower@spjimr.org.
  • Round 2
    • Shortlisted teams will be asked to present the complete implementation and feasibility of their solutions to the jury in a campus round. These teams will get a chance to work on their solution after round 1. The presentation would be followed by questions from the judges, which would be part of the evaluation.

Operations has evolved over the decades from just being manufacturing and production, permeating into every function of an organization ranging from logistics, information management, human resources, accounting, supply chain, quality and the like. With the evolution of technology, operational processes are racing to meet the growing demand and competition. Post World War II, operational research sciences dominated the need for optimization of processes in various functional areas.

The baton in the race, is now in the hands of strategic and tactical operationalization with the high involvement of information management systems. Businesses today talk about knitting operational strategy with Business strategy. Flipkart designed its business strategy based on its distribution channel. Toyota is the largest automobile manufacturer today, owing to the breakthrough operational excellence. Which operational key can unlock the gates of success for an organization? Is it strategy? Is it logistics? Or is it Quality?

Through “OperAce”, let us get an answer from the young and innovative minds to the question that lies before us, “What does it require to Ace the Race, in the world of operations management?”
