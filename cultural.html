Chhal, Shadyantra, Tyag, Pratishodh, Vijay

Do these verses take you back to the golden era of Mahabharata? Do these trigger your emotions and give you a feel of the times when you wanted to master the world and change your destiny? Do these give you a vision in which the terrifying ambiguities of morality are somehow resolved? Do these take you through the instances where you had to fight for your possession?

Then this is where you belong. Hit the stage. Act, dance, sing, shout and make the world listen to you. Enter the ‘Chkaravyuh’ and get a chance to showcase your talent. Epic was created 5000 years ago and another is on its way.

Duryodhana & Shakuni’s Chhal & Shadyantra, Gandhari’s Tyag, Bheema’s Pratishodh and Pandava’s Vijay still hold a relevance in today’s world. This January, we bring to you a platform to use the themes mentioned above, weave a story around it and make the people believe in you. Come. Perform. Create magic.

  • The duration of the video must not be more than 10 minutes. The band can cover more than one track in the video but the time must be adhered to.

  • Teams are requested to not use instrumental tracks and other kind of background backings in their video performance.

EVENT THEME

The Mayasabha was a hall par excellence where the floors gave the illusion of a pond and the ponds looked like mirrors. Hallways appeared to be where there were walls and vice versa. The columns of this palace were made of gold.

It is wonderfully symbolic of our obsession with the material world, the ego, and the now. It is certainly not uncommon for our lives to engulf us, as the hall of illusions did Duryodhana, leading to a lifetime of material desire, self-ignorance, egotism and general confusion.

Let us derive our meanings out of the fable and paint the canvas golden, or is it black?

“Dance till the stars come down from the rafters. Dance, Dance, Dance till you drop.” ~W.H. Auden

As the curtains raise this Ojas, and the audience waits with bated breath, it’s time to leave all your inhibitions behind and arouse the dancer within you. “Center Stage” is a dance extravaganza where teams will try to outclass their competitors with their captivating rhythm as they perform a variety of styles from Bollywood to Contemporary to Hiphop.

Round 1 : Eliminator

  • Each team is required to send a dance video (1-3 mins) of their performance while registering for the event.
  • The video needs to be uploaded on youtube and the link needs to be sent to ojas.centerstage@spjimr.org with the subject as <"TeamName_InstituteName">.
  • The video also has to be uploaded on the SPJIMR Ojas Facebook page. The number of likes received for the video on our page will form a small component in the short listing process.
  • The last day for submission of entries is 09th January, 2014.
  • Qualified teams go to the next round.
  • 8 teams will be shortlisted for the campus round.

Round 2 : Part A:Group Dance

  • Minimum 6 dancers on stage at any time
  • No lead dancer or solo performance
  • All forms of dance allowed
  • Weightage for costumes, music, choreography and coordination
  • Negative marking on exceeding time limit
  • Disqualification on exceeding number of participants
  • Prior permission required for usage of Props. No marks for the props.
  • Get your own CD/Pendrive with music for the dance

Round 2 : Part B : Adapt to the Music

  • The music will keep on changing. The participants need to adapt to the change of beat.
  • Organizers will provide music on the SPOT.
  • At least 4 dancers should be on the stage at any time.
  • Weightage will be given to the adaptation to change of music and beats, coordination and choreography.
  • Props not allowed in this round.

  • Maximum of 10 participants per team
  • Eliminator followed by Finals on campus
  • Only one team per college is allowed.
  • Participants must use more than one song in their performance.
  • Usage of props is allowed. These have to be brought by the teams themselves but the organizers hold the discretion of allowing it on stage. The losses/ damages to props is not a responsibility of the organizers.
  • Teams are requested to submit their props list prior to the competition and props have to be shown during practice slot. (props will not be allowed if you fail to produce them in practice slots)
  • Pre-recorded music should be brought in a pen drive in .mp3 or .wav format only (Other formats may not be supported on the computer – use them at your own risk).
  • Teams must follow the time limits. Teams shall be penalized by organizers for not doing so.
  • Any kind of fluid, flame, powders or heavy object is not allowed on stage.
  • Obscenity of any kind is not allowed and will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • The decision of the judges shall be final and binding.
  • The Terms and Conditions of participation are subject to change at any time without specific notice. The amended Terms and Conditions of entry will be effective immediately upon being posted on the website. It is the sole responsibility of the teams to regularly visit the Website to read any changes
