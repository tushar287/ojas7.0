Always been intrigued by Arnab Goswami? Well, we give you an opportunity to prove your mettle in this Mahayudh!

Final Word, the debating event of Ojas 7.0 exalts participants to bring out the great debater in them and win this war of words.

Every single voice can cause disruptive turbulence!

Format of the debate

Round 1:
  • Team size: 2 members of the same institute.
  • Online submission round.
  • Teams will be required to upload a video of the participants wherein one will speak in favor of the motion while the other will speak against the motion.
  • There should be only one video per team.
  • The video should not be longer than 3 minutes.
  • Videos will be judged on the basis of originality, quality of content, delivery and impact
  • The videos should be uploaded on Youtube and the link has to be submitted to ojas.thefinalword@spjimr.org. Naming convention: _.
  • Profanity/obscenity is strictly prohibited in any submitted content.
Round 2:
  • This round will be held on campus.
  • Draws will decide the opponents for the teams in Round 2. Topics will be given to each pair of teams and participants have to prepare both ‘For’ and ‘Against’ the motion for the topic allotted.
  • There will be two debaters per side. Each debater gets one turn to talk. This is the order of the speeches
  • Proposition constructive (The Prime Minister) – 5 minutes
    • This speaker will make a case for the motion for debate.
  • Opposition constructive (Leader of Opposition) – 5 minutes
    • This speaker will make arguments against the motion and can refute the proposition’s major points.
  • Opposition rebuttal (Member of Opposition) – 3 minutes
    • This speaker must put the debate together and explain why, given one or more arguments in the debate, the opposition team should win the debate. This speaker accounts for or refutes the proposition’s major points. No new arguments can be given.
  • Proposition rebuttal (Member of Government) – 3 minutes
  • This speaker should summarize the issues in the debate and explain why, even with the opposition’s arguments; the proposition teams should win the debate. This speaker accounts for all remaining major points of the opposition team. No new arguments shall be introduced.
  • 1st January: The topic of Round 1 will be released.
  • 8th Jan EOD: Entries to Round 1 close.
  • Round 2 will be held on campus between 24th to 26th January. Final date will be communicated to the short listed teams.

Operations has evolved over the decades from just being manufacturing and production, permeating into every function of an organization ranging from logistics, information management, human resources, accounting, supply chain, quality and the like. With the evolution of technology, operational processes are racing to meet the growing demand and competition. Post World War II, operational research sciences dominated the need for optimization of processes in various functional areas.

The baton in the race, is now in the hands of strategic and tactical operationalization with the high involvement of information management systems. Businesses today talk about knitting operational strategy with Business strategy. Flipkart designed its business strategy based on its distribution channel. Toyota is the largest automobile manufacturer today, owing to the breakthrough operational excellence. Which operational key can unlock the gates of success for an organization? Is it strategy? Is it logistics? Or is it Quality?

Through “OperAce”, let us get an answer from the young and innovative minds to the question that lies before us, “What does it require to Ace the Race, in the world of operations management?”
