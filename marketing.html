Ad-renalin will be a ‘brand’ new event of OJAS 7.0 which will test the advertising acumen of participants. The participants will be given a particular product/social initiative/programme on which they have to create an advertisement campaign, with an analysis of the target group, key touch points and assess the impact of the same.

The competition will have 2 rounds.
  1. Round 1: Online submission of entries. Deliverables will be
    • Long Copy Ad/Poster
    • 1 page concept note explaining the idea behind the ad, target group, etc.
    The AdMania facebook page will post each entry and there will be a 30% weightage for number of likes for the respective posts.
  2. Round 2: Based on the entries, we will shortlist 6 teams for on campus round. The participants will have to come prepared with the following deliverables:
    • 60 sec Video Ad
    • PowerPoint Presentation (explaining the idea behind the video ad)
    • 20 sec Radio Ad (Maybe on the spot with an hour to prepare depending on logistical constraints)
    The video will be judged out of 30 marks, PPT out of 10, the radio ad out of 10 giving a consolidated score of 50. The team with the highest consolidated score will be declared the winner.

Rules & Regulations

  • The event is open to Business-school students only.
  • Team size: 3-4 members.
  • Members should be from the same institute.
  • One participant cannot be a part of more than one team for a particular event.
  • Multiple teams from the same college are allowed.
  • All decisions in matters of eligibility, authenticity and final judgments will be with Organizing Committee, SPJIMR, Mumbai.

Round 1: Goes live on 03/01/2015.
Submission of round 1: 08/01/2015
Results of round 1: 10/01/2015

Focus of every Marketer is: “Putting the right product in the right place, at the right price, at the right time”.

And for that you need to ‘Know Your Customer’.

A product is seen as an item that satisfies what a consumer demands. It is a tangible good or an intangible service. A company will only sell what the consumer specifically wants to buy. So, marketers should study consumer wants and needs in order to attract them one by one with something he/she wants to purchase. While promotion is "manipulative" and from the seller, communication is "cooperative" and from the buyer with the aim to create a dialogue with the potential customers based on their needs and lifestyles. Marketers should know how the target market prefers to buy, how to be there and be ubiquitous, in order to guarantee convenience to buy.

If you get just one element wrong, it can spell disaster. You could be left promoting a car with amazing fuel-economy in a country where fuel is very cheap

Concept: The purpose of conducting KYC is to test the marketer’s acumen in selling a new technology product to the local Indian masses.
