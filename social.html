A unique fusion of western principles of efficiency and Eastern ethos

M-Power demands students to use their business acumen to provide sustainable and tangible solutions for social issues in India. The Theme for this year is "Inclusion".

Theme for this Year: The theme for this year is "Inclusion". As time passes by, we see there are two India’s emerging, urban and rural, with disparity between them increasing each passing second. Rural India while home to majority of the population, contributes very little to the GDP. It lags behind its Urban brother be it in Banking, Mobile Penetration or Internet Penetration. All this while Tier-I cities come under strain and are beginning to saturate. As India wakes up to the importance of Inclusive growth and its impact on the social fabric, This OJAS... Let’s find out ways to grow together.

Challenge: We would like participants to give us innovative ideas which corporates can undertake to improve the quality of rural facilities and infrastructure for the benefit of rural India. What must you do?

  1. Identify the major problems which are causing the rural-urban divide. These problems can be spread across the areas which you think are important. You are expected to choose broad areas like Telecom (TMT), Consumer Goods, Financial Services, IT, Infrastructure etc
  2. Suggest solutions for rural inclusiveness in the area that you have selected.
  3. The submission must include the problems faced by rural areas in this sector and feasible solutions. Quality and depth of solutions will form the primary basis for evaluation, along with the issues identified and the overall approach taken.
  4. You may choose to explore multiple problems across multiple sectors or deep dive into one sector and provide solutions for that sector alone. This is left to you!
  5. This time, MPower is an open-ended challenge and we would like students to think of creative means to solve this problem.

Concept: The purpose of conducting MPower is to utilize the creativity and business acumen of Indian students to come up with sustainable and tangible solutions to the social issues of India.

The event comprises of two rounds:
  • Round 1
    • Participating teams would need to submit a Power Point presentation of 8 slides (including an executive summary and excluding the cover page). The presentation should cover an overview of the suggested ideas and solution. The presentations need to be mail to ojas.mpower@spjimr.org.
  • Round 2
    • Shortlisted teams will be asked to present the complete implementation and feasibility of their solutions to the jury in a campus round. These teams will get a chance to work on their solution after round 1. The presentation would be followed by questions from the judges, which would be part of the evaluation.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

  • The event is open to students from all B-Schools.
  • Participant in a team can be either first year students or second year students, but should be from the same institute and no participant can be a part of more than one team.
  • Teams should comprise of minimum 2 and maximum 3 members.
  • Only one entry would be allowed per team for the competition.
  • No changes in team composition are allowed after registration.
  • Details of Round 2 will be uploaded after announcement of results of Round 1.
  • Participants are requested to like the Facebook Page and join the event for regular updates.
  • The decisions of the Judges will be final and binding on the participants. The Terms and Conditions of participation are subject to change at any time without specific notice. The amended Terms and Conditions of entry will be effective immediately upon being posted on the website. It is the sole responsibility of the teams to regularly visit the website to read any changes.

  2. Deadline for first round submission is 7th January 2015.
  3. Further instructions will be provided to shortlisted teams.

The 14-year Vanvaas of Pandavas came along with the rider of living incognito for one year without being recognised. They took shelter in Matsyadesh– Yudhisthir disguised as a minister, Bheem as a cook, Arjun as the princess’ dance teacher, Draupadi as a beautician and so on. Living among the masses and sharing their struggle and concern, they learned the art of success, learned the humility and struggle that a greater motive demands.

A hero’s life always filled with challenges. Even the greatest Heroes in the history of mankind had to live identity-less among the masses and sweat and toil in the harshest environment. This is where people find their destiny. Their path to glory is paved here

India is a country with more than 1.2 billion people with a huge proportion of them living below the poverty line. These people faces a myriad of challenges like poverty, unemployment, low agricultural productivity, poor education, inhumane living conditions and many more. We need people to think keeping these issues at the centre and come up with a sustainable and an achievable Social Business Plan which should solve a social problem of the country.

We challenge the participants to dissolve among the unknowns, identify their problems and find an opportunity in those bleakest scenarios. This is where the leaders of future will be born and become the face of the world

Teams selected for the second round would be provided with a unique opportunity to visit the biggest slums of Asia : DHARAVI. This would be a guided tour unearthing the social problems of such slums.
